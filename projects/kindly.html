THE BRIEF //

Manhattan Associates deploys warehouse and supply-chain software to enterprise clients across retail, life sciences, and healthcare. Before go-live, consultants manually reformat training decks, slide by slide, for each client's processes and terminology. The bet: an internal tool that ingests source materials and auto-generates tailored onboarding content.

DISCOVERY //

Shadowed consultants across 3 concurrent client engagements and mapped the prep workflow end to end — where materials came from, how long reformatting took, and what still had to be hand-edited before a client review. The pain wasn't writing content from scratch; it was repackaging the same knowledge into client-specific formats under tight deployment timelines.

what we shipped...

DEFINING THE MVP //

Scoped an AI pipeline that accepts training documents and video, extracts key procedures, and outputs draft slide decks and handouts aligned to a client's vocabulary. Cut scope on full LMS integration and real-time collaboration — consultants needed a fast first draft they could edit, not a polished final deck on day one.

VALIDATION //

Piloted with consultants on active engagements. Measured prep time before and after — manual formatting dropped by 80%. Iterated on output templates when reviewers flagged jargon mismatches or missing process steps. If a generated section couldn't survive a real client walkthrough, it didn't stay in the default template.

WHAT I OWNED //

Workflow research, MVP scoping, stakeholder alignment with consulting and engineering leads, and shipping the first usable version. Stayed close enough to deployment reality that the tool reflected how consultants actually prep — not how a slide deck looks in a demo.
