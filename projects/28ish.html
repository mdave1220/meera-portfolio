Menstrual Cycle Insight App

UX Design Product Management Frontend Engineering Georgia Tech Capstone 1 Year Project

Project Overview

A mobile app designed for women to learn more about their menstrual cycle through personalized insights. Our client, 28ish, tasked us with creating a new app that tracks a woman's menstrual cycle and provides comparative insights from one phase to another.

Goal: Empower women to be more informed about their menstrual cycle with comparative insights across phases.

Research & Discovery

We sent out a survey to 50+ women to understand what they felt was wrong with existing period tracking apps, how informed they felt on their menstrual cycle, and what they'd want to see in a new app.

Key Findings:

  • Most women didn't feel properly educated on their cycle
  • Users wanted to learn more about how to capitalize off the different phases
  • Desired insights on how phases affect mood, social life, productivity, and energy

Wireframing & Design Process

I analyzed existing period tracking apps, such as Flo and Apple Health, to understand typical calendar tracking heuristics and behaviors. From there, I created low-fidelity screens for:

  • Daily check-in page
  • Home screen with calendar view
  • Insights pages with comparative data

Prototyping & Development

I finalized a functional prototype in Figma. 28ish had their own design system, but I helped refine it to work seamlessly with the TamaGUI component library for development.

I also studied the React-Native-Charts library to understand its structure and created the insights pages based on that knowledge, ensuring a smooth transition from design to development.

Product Management & Development

Beyond UX and design, I was heavily involved as a frontend engineer and product manager for this capstone internship:

  • Project Management: Monitored a Jira board with user story tickets and created a 5-sprint iteration plan
  • Client Relations: Led weekly meetings with the client to show design progress and align goals
  • Team Leadership: Oversaw progress among the development team to ensure client satisfaction
  • Frontend Development: Worked directly with TamaGUI components and React-Native-Charts library

Key Takeaways

Client Feedback is Crucial

Leading weekly meetings with the client while acting as both UX designer and product manager helped me discover a new passion for taking products from start to finish, beyond just UX.

Design-to-Development Transition

Initially, I was discouraged by the extra time needed to switch Figma UI components to match what our developers were using. However, I realized how crucial this seamless transition is for project efficiency.

Product Management x UX

Before this internship, I didn't think I wanted to go into product management. Having the opportunity to learn Agile development and lead project timelines allowed me to explore this new field and combine it with my UX passions.
