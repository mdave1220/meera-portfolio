Travel Jam

Smart Trip Planner for Study Abroad Students

UX Research UI Design Figma User Testing 6-week Project

The Problem

When studying abroad, students often want to travel in groups but find it difficult to coordinate trips. Each person may have different budgets, time constraints, and interests — leading to inefficient, stressful planning across multiple platforms.

We aimed to design a solution that makes trip planning intuitive, collaborative, and personalized for the needs of students traveling in Europe.

Research & Discovery

Research Goal: Identify the key challenges study abroad students face when planning trips.

Our Process: To understand our users, we conducted primary interviews with seven study abroad students and created a user survey to capture travel habits, planning challenges, and desired features.

Key Insights:

  • Students prioritize convenience, cost, and time when planning short trips
  • Most users use multiple apps (Google Sheets, Skyscanner, Maps, TikTok) to cobble together a plan
  • Planning in groups is hard — especially when balancing different priorities
  • Students want quick suggestions based on time, cost, and interest alignment

Ideation & Design Strategy

We brainstormed around key themes: simplicity, personalization, and collaboration. Our core ideas focused on:

  • Smart itinerary recommendations based on budget, group size, and interests
  • A shared platform for group input and agreement
  • Minimizing the number of apps needed to finalize a trip

Wireframing & Design Process

We sketched a few interface ideas, focusing on an intuitive, mobile-first flow. The app would:

  • Ask simple, key planning questions (budget, interests, timeframe)
  • Show grouped destination options with estimated cost + travel time
  • Let users vote and finalize the itinerary within the app

Final Prototype

We designed a mobile prototype in Figma, emphasizing simplicity and personalization. Key features included:

  • Home Screen: Users input trip preferences (budget, duration, group size)
  • Destination Cards: Show top matches with visuals, price breakdowns, and key highlights
  • Group Vote Feature: For collaborative decision-making
  • Final Screen: Shareable itinerary (flights, hotels, to-do list)

Key Takeaways

Figma Mastery

I learned the basics of Figma from taking on this project in my user interface course. It was initially a lot of trial and error, but learning how to create reusable components, autolayout, layout grids, and apply a mini-design system was a blast.

User Research Importance

This project helped me understand the value of user-centered design in fast-paced, real-life contexts. I learned how to translate ambiguous problems into structured flows, how to design for collaboration, and how to prioritize features that genuinely solve a user need.

Design Process

I built my foundation in Figma and UI design systems through this project. I learned how to convert vague problems into structured design flows. The experience showed me how user research directly informs features that solve real pain points.
