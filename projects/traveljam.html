THE PROBLEM //

Study-abroad students burn hours planning trips, juggling group interests, budgets, and time constraints, usually ending in compromise fatigue.

DISCOVERY //

Participant observation in real group planning sessions + 1:1 interviews. Students average $20–50 per activity, spend multiple sessions planning, and transportation logistics were the recurring pain.

THE BET //

A swipeable discovery feed filtered by budget, interest, and location, betting a familiar TikTok-style mental model would flatten the learning curve. Explored three lo-fi directions before committing to a high-fidelity Figma prototype.

VALIDATION //

7 moderated think-alouds + 20-person between-subjects study (lo-fi vs hi-fi, t-test). The hi-fi version did not significantly raise satisfaction. Instead it surfaced discoverability, affordance, and hierarchy issues that polish alone couldn't fix.

WHAT I LEARNED //

Next iteration: simplified homepage hierarchy, first-use swipe affordance, clearer navigation, preference-capturing onboarding. A familiar pattern only works with clear affordances.
